Uncertainty-Calibrated Trust Regions for Query-Efficient QAOA Parameter Search

For low-depth implementations of the Quantum Approximate Optimization Algorithm (QAOA), the dominant practical cost is the number of objective-function queries required to identify useful variational parameters. UQ-QAOA converts graph-conditioned predictive uncertainty into an operational trust-region geometry: a Graph Isomorphism Network predicts a diagonal Gaussian over the QAOA angles, and four information sources—neural predictor, local $k$-nearest-neighbour prior, global population prior, and a trotterized quantum-annealing schedule—are fused by precision-weighted combination to obtain a posterior mean and anisotropic step sizes for sequential coordinate refinement. This page presents the method in full mathematical detail, the reproducible exact-statevector evidence that supports it, and a companion graph-learning program spanning quantum optimization and biomedical inference.

Problem Setting

QAOA, MaxCut, and the objective-query model

Let $G=(V,E)$ be an undirected graph with $n=|V|$ vertices. The MaxCut cost Hamiltonian, the depth-$p$ QAOA state with mixer $H_M=\sum_i X_i$, and the exact objective are

$$H_C(G)=\sum_{(i,j)\in E}\frac{1-Z_iZ_j}{2},\qquad \lvert\psi_G(\theta)\rangle=\prod_{\ell=1}^{p} e^{-i\beta_\ell H_M}\,e^{-i\gamma_\ell H_C(G)}\,\lvert+\rangle^{\otimes n},\qquad f_G(\theta)=\langle\psi_G(\theta)\rvert H_C(G)\lvert\psi_G(\theta)\rangle.$$

The parameter vector is $\theta=(\gamma_1,\dots,\gamma_p,\beta_1,\dots,\beta_p)\in\Theta_p=[0,2\pi)^p\times[0,\pi)^p$, and the approximation ratio is $r_G(\theta)=f_G(\theta)/C_G^\star$ with optimal cut value $C_G^\star$. Cost is measured in objective queries $Q=\#\{\theta\text{ evaluated}\}$, and performance is reported through the best-so-far query curve

$$Q\;\longmapsto\;\max_{1\le q\le Q} r_G(\theta_q),\qquad\text{with}\qquad Q_G(\tau)=\min\{q: r_G^{\mathrm{best}}(q)\ge\tau\}.$$

In a measurement-limited regime, a method that reaches comparable quality with substantially fewer queries can be more useful than one that attains a marginally higher objective only after many more evaluations. UQ-QAOA is designed for precisely this regime.

Method

Graph-conditioned Gaussian prediction and four-source posterior fusion

A Graph Isomorphism Network (GIN) with learnable $\varepsilon$, layer-normalized two-layer MLPs, and spectral positional features performs message passing, then a Gaussian head emits a mean and a diagonal covariance over QAOA angles:

$$h_i^{(\ell+1)}=\mathrm{MLP}^{(\ell)}\!\Big((1+\varepsilon^{(\ell)})\,h_i^{(\ell)}+\!\!\sum_{j\in\mathcal N(i)}\!\tilde A_{ij}\,h_j^{(\ell)}\Big),\quad \tilde A=D^{-1}(A+I),\qquad q_\phi(\theta\mid G)=\mathcal N\!\big(\mu_\phi(G),\,\Sigma_\phi(G)\big).$$

The covariance head is $\Sigma_\phi(G)=\mathrm{diag}\big(\sigma_{\min}^2+\mathrm{softplus}(a_\phi(G))\big)$, trained by Gaussian negative log-likelihood with weight decay. The NLL simultaneously rewards accurate prediction and penalizes covariance inflation:

$$\mathcal L_{\mathrm{nll}}(\phi)=\frac{1}{2N}\sum_{i=1}^{N}\sum_{k=1}^{2p}\left[\frac{\big(\theta^{\mathrm{ref}}_{i,k}-\mu_{\phi,k}(G_i)\big)^2}{\sigma_{\phi,k}^2(G_i)}+\log\sigma_{\phi,k}^2(G_i)\right]+\eta_{\mathrm{wd}}\lVert\phi\rVert_2^2.$$

Four independent sources—the GIN predictor, a local $k{=}5$ nearest-neighbour prior, a global population prior, and a trotterized quantum-annealing (TQA) prior—are fused by precision-weighted (inverse-variance) combination, where sources with smaller predicted variance exert stronger pull on the joint estimate:

$$\Sigma_{\mathrm{post}}^{-1}={\Sigma_{\mathrm{GIN}}'}^{-1}+\Sigma_{\mathrm{loc}}^{-1}+\Sigma_{\mathrm{glob}}^{-1}+\Sigma_{\mathrm{TQA}}^{-1},\qquad \mu_{\mathrm{post}}=\Sigma_{\mathrm{post}}\sum_{s}\Sigma_s^{-1}\mu_s,$$

with $\Sigma_{\mathrm{GIN}}'=\max(\Sigma_{\mathrm{GIN}},\Sigma_{\mathrm{loc}})$ a calibration floor. The posterior covariance then defines the anisotropic trust region and the per-coordinate step sizes that govern refinement:

$$\mathcal T_{\phi,\rho}(G)=\Big\{\theta\in\Theta_p:(\theta-\mu_\phi)^{\!\top}(\Sigma_\phi+\lambda I)^{-1}(\theta-\mu_\phi)\le\rho^2\Big\},\qquad \delta_j=\mathrm{clip}\!\big(0.5\sqrt{[\Sigma_{\mathrm{post}}]_{jj}},\,0.05,\,0.30\big).$$
Phase 0 / 2a

TQA safety prefix

A short trotterized-annealing prefix makes the policy dominance-preserving: at each early query count, UQ-QAOA has evaluated the same TQA candidate available to the strongest physics-informed baseline before spending budget on learned geometry.

Phase 1 / 2b

Posterior-anchor evaluation

Nonduplicate deterministic anchors—TQA, posterior mean, GIN mean, local $k$-NN median, and global median—are evaluated, and the empirical best becomes the refinement incumbent.

Phase 2c

Greedy coordinate refinement

For each dimension $j$ the search probes $\pm\delta_j$ from the incumbent, updating on strict improvement; a full pass without improvement halves the step sizes (trust-region contraction).

Guarantees

When a learned trust region saves queries

The guarantees are deliberately local and conditional: they formalize when a calibrated, compact trust region reduces objective queries, not global QAOA optimality.

Theorem — Exact-query best-of-$K$ guarantee

Suppose $K$ candidates are drawn independently from a proposal supported on $\mathcal T_{\phi,\rho}(G)$ that places mass at least $\alpha_G(\varepsilon)>0$ on the $\varepsilon$-optimal subset $\mathcal A_{G,\varepsilon}=\{\theta\in\mathcal T: f_G(\theta)\ge f_G^{\mathcal T}-\varepsilon\}$. Then

so $K\ge\log(1/\delta)/\alpha_G(\varepsilon)$ suffices for $f_G(\widehat\theta_K)\ge f_G^{\mathcal T}-\varepsilon$ with probability at least $1-\delta$. For an ellipsoidal region with locally $L_G$-Lipschitz objective, $\alpha_G(\varepsilon)\gtrsim (\varepsilon/L_G)^d\big/\big(\rho^d\det(\Sigma_\phi+\lambda I)^{1/2}\big)$ — a tighter calibrated covariance and smaller radius reduce the number of candidates needed to hit the near-optimal set.

Proposition — Finite-sample conformal coverage

For exchangeable validation scores $s(G)=(\theta^*-\mu_\phi)^{\!\top}(\Sigma_\phi+\lambda I)^{-1}(\theta^*-\mu_\phi)$ with $\hat q_{1-\alpha}$ the $\lceil(M+1)(1-\alpha)\rceil$-th order statistic over $M$ calibration instances, a new exchangeable test instance satisfies

$$\mathbb P\!\big[\theta^*(G_{M+1})\in\mathcal T_{\hat q_{1-\alpha}}(G_{M+1})\big]\ge 1-\alpha.$$

A finite-shot extension adds a sub-Gaussian union bound over $S$-shot estimates, separating parameter-query cost from shot cost.

Companion Project • Graph AI across domains

Transcriptomic QAOA initialization and biomedical CTG screening

The same graph-conditioned interface—graph in, calibrated parameterization out—is exercised in two domains. A graph-conditioned GNN predicts depth-2 QAOA angles for weighted MaxCut on prostate transcriptomic co-expression graphs, reaching near-direct-search quality at a single fast forward pass; a residual clinical GCN on a cardiotocography patient-similarity graph performs node-level pathologic-risk prediction. The studies below report held-out evidence and its bounded operating regime.

Taken together, the companion results identify structure-aware parameter prediction as a viable route to transferring fixed-depth quantum optimization across related weighted instances, and show the same formulation transfers to a biomedical graph setting—within a bounded operating regime that the work makes explicit.

Comparative Results

The comparison is framed against direct depth-2 search and simpler learned initializers. The strongest claim is retention of the downstream MaxCut objective on held-out transcriptomic graphs with sharply lower inference-time cost.

From Benchmarks to Applications

The benchmark is organized around repeated fixed-depth optimization on structured weighted graphs. In that setting, a better model is not merely numerically stronger. It changes how often full parameter search must be rerun, how cheaply related instances can be evaluated, and how confidently objective retention can be audited.

  • In transcriptomic optimization, the graph-conditioned result means new related biological graphs can be evaluated without rerunning expensive multi-start search from scratch, while staying close to the direct-search objective.
  • In noise-aware evaluation, the supporting result means the learned initialization remains close to the classical-search baseline as local depolarizing noise increases across the tested grid.
  • In the secondary CTG setting, the same structural learning approach can be reused for cohort-level decision tasks, even though that branch is not the primary contribution of the page.
