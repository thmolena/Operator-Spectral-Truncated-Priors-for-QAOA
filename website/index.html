UQ-QAOA

Uncertainty-Calibrated Trust Regions for Query-Efficient QAOA Parameter Search

At low circuit depth, the cost of running the Quantum Approximate Optimization Algorithm is dominated not by wall-clock time but by the number of objective-function queries spent locating useful variational parameters. UQ-QAOA reframes predictive uncertainty as the controller of that search: a Graph Isomorphism Network maps a problem graph to a Gaussian over QAOA angles, and the predicted covariance — rather than the mean alone — is repurposed as operational trust-region geometry. Four information sources are combined by a closed-form, precision-weighted fusion into a posterior mean and anisotropic per-coordinate step sizes, turning a learned distribution into an explicit, query-efficient policy for where to center, how widely to probe each angle, and how to spend a fixed budget.

Background and Motivation

The scientific setting and the open problem

QAOA solves combinatorial optimization by tuning a parameterized quantum circuit through a classical outer loop. On near-term, measurement-limited hardware, the binding constraint is not runtime but the objective-query count — the number of distinct angle vectors that must be evaluated on the processor or simulator. Every method that reduces this count without sacrificing solution quality directly extends what is feasible at a given hardware cost, which makes query efficiency the central figure of merit for the outer loop.

The resource-aware question

Under a fixed query budget, can graph-conditioned uncertainty distribute those evaluations more effectively than single-source warm starts or generic black-box optimizers? The question is consequential because barren plateaus and noise blunt gradient-based outer loops at low depth, so the gains must come from spending fewer evaluations more intelligently rather than from cheaper gradients.

The structural premise that makes learning possible

At fixed depth and within bounded-degree graph families, optimal QAOA angles concentrate and transfer across related instances. This regularity is what a learned policy can exploit, confining search to the region where good parameters are known to lie rather than exploring blindly. The advance is not that a GNN predicts angles — that capability already exists — but that its predicted covariance, ordinarily discarded as a confidence score, is reinterpreted as the search geometry itself.

Mathematical Preliminaries

Formal objects and notation

The following objects, transcribed verbatim from the manuscript, fix the notation used throughout.

MaxCut cost Hamiltonian on \(G=(V,E)\), \(n=|V|\)
\[ H_C(G)=\sum_{(i,j)\in E}\frac{1-Z_iZ_j}{2}, \qquad H_M=\sum_{i=1}^{n}X_i. \]
QAOA state and exact objective at depth \(p\), \(\theta=(\gamma_1,\dots,\gamma_p,\beta_1,\dots,\beta_p)\in\Theta_p\)
\[ \ket{\psi_G(\theta)}=\prod_{\ell=1}^{p}e^{-i\beta_\ell H_M}e^{-i\gamma_\ell H_C(G)}\ket{+}^{\otimes n}, \qquad f_G(\theta)=\bra{\psi_G(\theta)}H_C(G)\ket{\psi_G(\theta)}. \]

Parameter domain \(\Theta_p=[0,2\pi)^p\times[0,\pi)^p\subset\mathbb{R}^{2p}\); approximation ratio \(r_G(\theta)=f_G(\theta)/C_G^\star\).

Finite-shot estimator (cost of one query is one objective evaluation)
\[ \widehat{f}_{G,S}(\theta)=\frac1S\sum_{a=1}^{S}C_G(z_a), \qquad z_a\sim|\braket{z_a}{\psi_G(\theta)}|^2, \qquad Q=\#\{\theta\text{ evaluated}\}. \]
Graph-conditioned predictive distribution (GIN head) and induced trust region
\[ q_\phi(\theta\mid G)=\mathcal{N}\!\big(\mu_\phi(G),\Sigma_\phi(G)\big), \quad \mathcal{T}_{\phi,\rho}(G)=\Big\{\theta\in\Theta_p:\ (\theta-\mu_\phi(G))^{\!\mathsf{T}}(\Sigma_\phi(G)+\lambda I)^{-1}(\theta-\mu_\phi(G))\le\rho^2\Big\}. \]
Precision-weighted four-source posterior (GIN′, local k-NN, global, TQA)
\[ \Sigma_{\mathrm{post}}^{-1}={\Sigma_{\mathrm{GIN}}'}^{-1}+\Sigma_{\mathrm{loc}}^{-1}+\Sigma_{\mathrm{glob}}^{-1}+\Sigma_{\mathrm{TQA}}^{-1}, \qquad \delta_j=\mathrm{clip}\!\big(0.5\sqrt{[\Sigma_{\mathrm{post}}]_{jj}},\,0.05,\,0.30\big). \]
Trotterized quantum annealing prior (physics-informed, test-instance-free)
\[ \gamma_\ell^{\mathrm{TQA}}=\frac{\ell}{p}\,\Delta t,\qquad \beta_\ell^{\mathrm{TQA}}=\Big(1-\frac{\ell}{p}\Big)\Delta t,\qquad \Delta t=T/p. \]
Problem Formulation

Precise problem statement

Fix QAOA depth \(p\) and an objective-query budget \(Q\). For a test graph \(G\), a probabilistic predictor \(h_\phi:G\mapsto(\mu_\phi(G),\Sigma_\phi(G))\) emits a Gaussian over angles, which in turn defines an ellipsoidal trust region \(\mathcal{T}_{\phi,\rho}(G)\). The task is then a budgeted local search inside that region,

\[ \max_{\theta\in\mathcal{T}_{\phi,\rho}(G)} f_G(\theta)\quad\text{using at most }Q\text{ objective queries,} \]

Performance is read off the query-performance curve \(Q\mapsto\max_{1\le q\le Q}r_G(\theta_q)\) and the query count \(Q_G(\tau)=\min\{q:r_G^{\mathrm{best}}(q)\ge\tau\}\) required to reach a target ratio \(\tau\). Within this formulation the predicted distribution plays two distinct roles: its mean supplies the warm start, while its covariance supplies the geometry — fixing where to center, how wide to probe each coordinate, and how to apportion the remaining budget. The learned distributional object thus becomes the full search policy, not merely an initializer.

Principal Contributions

Complete claims

  1. Uncertainty as search geometry. The graph-conditioned covariance is treated not as a confidence score to be reported but as the metric that defines anisotropic trust-region scales and the per-coordinate step sizes of refinement — reducing a learned distribution to three concrete decisions: where to center, how wide to probe each coordinate, and how to allocate a fixed query budget.
  2. Posterior fusion of learned and physics-informed priors. Four heterogeneous sources — a GIN predictor, a local \(k{=}5\)-nearest-neighbour prior, a global population prior, and a TQA prior — are merged into a single posterior mean and diagonal covariance by one explicit, non-iterative, precision-weighted formula.
  3. Controlled matched- and dimension-scaled-budget evidence. The primary benchmark fixes \(p{=}3\) and compares methods along matched query curves; the \(p{=}4\)–7 experiments use dimension-scaled budgets \(Q_p=5+4p\). Every evaluation is exact-statevector on unweighted MaxCut, and all claims are scoped explicitly to this CPU-only regime.
  4. Fair local-search baselines and ablations. The strongest competitor, TQA, runs under the identical greedy coordinate-refinement protocol, removing protocol advantage as a confound. Accompanying ablations remove covariance, shuffle covariance, drop individual priors, or disable refinement, and four black-box optimizers (DE, CMA-ES, GP-EI, Nelder–Mead) compete under the same budget.
  5. Calibration and finite-shot diagnostics. Because uncertainty is the object of study, it is held to account: the work reports trust-region coverage, calibration diagnostics, and sensitivity under finite-shot and noisy objectives.
  6. Auditable computational artifact. Deterministic Python scripts regenerate every reported number, and a C++20 reference interface records the evaluator contract, compiler settings, and kernel structure, cleanly separating candidate generation from evaluator kernels so future accelerated backends can drop in.

Theoretical Guarantees

Formal statements, each with a one-line reading

Theorem 1 (Exact-query best-of-\(K\) guarantee). With samples drawn from a proposal on \(\mathcal{T}_{\phi,\rho}(G)\) placing mass \(\alpha_G(\varepsilon)>0\) on the \(\varepsilon\)-optimal subset, if \(K\ge\log(1/\delta)/\alpha_G(\varepsilon)\) then with probability \(\ge1-\delta\), \(f_G(\widehat\theta_K)\ge f_G^{\mathcal{T}}-\varepsilon\); more precisely \(\Pr[f_G(\widehat\theta_K)Interpretation: a calibrated trust region pays off only to the extent that it concentrates proposal mass on near-optimal angles; the single quantity \(\alpha_G(\varepsilon)\) governs query efficiency.
Theorem 2 (Finite-shot best-of-\(K\) guarantee). Under sub-Gaussian shot noise of proxy variance \(\sigma_G^2/S\) with \(\sigma_G^2\le|E|^2/4\), choosing \(K\ge\log(2/\delta)/\alpha_G(\varepsilon/2)\) and \(S\ge 32\sigma_G^2\log(4K/\delta)/\varepsilon^2\) yields \(f_G(\widehat\theta_K^{\mathrm{shot}})\ge f_G^{\mathcal{T}}-\varepsilon\) w.p. \(\ge1-\delta\).
Interpretation: query cost and shot cost decouple — few queries suffice to place good candidates, but many shots may be needed to rank them reliably under noise.
Proposition 1 (Finite-sample conformal coverage). With exchangeable validation/test instances and Mahalanobis nonconformity scores, the conformal radius \(\hat q_{1-\alpha}\) gives \(\mathbb{P}[\theta^*(G_{M+1})\in\mathcal{T}_{\hat q_{1-\alpha}}(G_{M+1})]\ge1-\alpha\).
Interpretation: the trust-region radius carries a distribution-free, finite-sample coverage guarantee, offered as an optional calibration mode.
Proposition 2 (Optimality of precision-weighted fusion). If each source reports \(\mu_s=\theta^\star+\varepsilon_s\), \(\varepsilon_s\sim\mathcal{N}(0,\Sigma_s)\) independent, then among linear unbiased estimators \(\sum_s W_s\mu_s\) with \(\sum_s W_s=I\), the precision weights \(W_s^\star=\Sigma_{\mathrm{post}}\Sigma_s^{-1}\) uniquely minimize the error covariance in the Loewner order, attaining \(\Sigma_{\mathrm{post}}\).
Interpretation: the fusion rule is provably the unique minimum-variance unbiased combiner (equivalently, the flat-prior posterior mean), certifying that no unbiased estimator centers more tightly.
Lemma 1 (Per-coordinate trust-region contraction). For diagonal sources, \([\Sigma_{\mathrm{post}}]_{jj}\le\min_s[\Sigma_s]_{jj}\) for every coordinate \(j\); hence the fused step size never exceeds that of the most confident single source, and the fused axis-aligned region is contained in every source’s.
Interpretation: fusion can only shrink the per-coordinate search scale, never enlarge it; this smaller volume enters Theorem 1 through \(\det(\Sigma_\phi+\lambda I)\) and lowers the queries required.
Proposition 3 (Convergence of greedy coordinate refinement). With \(f_G\) bounded (\(0\le f_G\le|E|\)), the incumbent value sequence of Phase 2c is nondecreasing and converges; a pass without strict improvement yields a \(\delta\)-approximate coordinate-wise local maximum, and step-halving drives \(\delta\to0\) so every limit point is coordinate-wise stationary wherever \(f_G\) is differentiable.
Interpretation: under the trust-region contraction rule the refinement loop is well-posed, and its incumbent converges to a coordinate-wise stationary point.

Significance and Impact

What changes for the field, and what is not claimed

Consequence

The work reframes graph-conditioned predictive uncertainty as a test-time resource-allocation object in its own right: the learned covariance builds the local search geometry and the dimension-wise step sizes, joining initialization quality to budget allocation through one explicit, theoretically certified mechanism. Operationally this matters because the policy never asks the quantum backend for gradients or Hessians and maintains no per-instance surrogate — every query in the budget buys a direct objective evaluation.

Stated limitations (kept visible)

  • All evidence is exact-statevector, CPU-only, on unweighted MaxCut at small \(n\le18\); no hardware or quantum-advantage claims are made.
  • The four sources are not strictly independent (k-NN and GIN share training data), so the fusion is an engineering approximation rather than an exact posterior; its value is justified empirically by the ablation.
  • The covariance is diagonal: cross-layer angle correlations and the torus topology of \(\Theta_p\) are not modeled, and wrapped-normal or full-covariance extensions remain future work.
  • At \(p{=}6,7\), where the budget is a minimal single-instance \(Q=5\), the methods are tightly clustered; no statistical claims are made at \(p>3\).
Reproducibility

A pip-installable artifact that reproduces every number

Nothing in the paper is hand-entered: every figure, table, and number is emitted by deterministic scripts under submission/code/. The artifact ships a modular Python package (python/uq_qaoa/) with a pyproject.toml, a C++20 reference statevector backend (cpp/), per-experiment entry points, YAML configurations for depths \(p{=}3\)–7, a unit-test suite, and the end-to-end drivers generate_all.py and reproduce.sh. 

# Obtain identical results from the public artifact
git clone https://github.com/thmolena/Hybrid-Quantum-Graph-AI-QAOA-GNN-Biomedical-Optimization
cd Hybrid-Quantum-Graph-AI-QAOA-GNN-Biomedical-Optimization/submission

pip install -e code/            # installs the uq_qaoa package (pyproject.toml)
bash code/reproduce.sh smoke    # one-command deterministic reproduction
python code/generate_all.py     # regenerate every figure and table

Determinism is not incidental but contractual: the backend fixes candidate ordering by seed and method, removes duplicate anchors before any budget is spent, wraps out-of-domain angles modularly, decrements the budget exactly once per unique evaluation, and pins a single floating-point reduction policy. Reference platform: Apple M2 Pro (10-core), 16 GB; Python 3.11.7, NumPy 1.26.4, SciPy 1.12.0. The repository ships the graph seeds, train/test splits, raw query traces, and figure source data; a DOI-bearing archival release will be created before final publication.
